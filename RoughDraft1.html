About

Traditional college teaching methods were revolutionized in 1970 when Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) became a pioneer of a project-based learning curriculum. This revamped curriculum, dubbed The WPI Plan,

“...features integrative project work across four years, both in the major and in general education, in classrooms, and around the globe at more than 40 project centers. Under the Plan, students work closely with faculty and each other to develop solutions to real-world problems in their own communities and in communities around the globe.” (Project Based Learning).

WPI’s Global Projects Program (GPP) provides the opportunity for students to tackle these “real-world problems” every year with its global project sites. Over 65% of students at WPI complete an Interactive Qualifying Project (IQP) at one of these global project sites during their undergraduate career, a 5% increase from the year prior (Interactive Qualifying Project). These projects help not only the students who complete them, but also the communities they pertain to, providing a measurable impact on all parties involved with the project process. The Princeton Review ranked WPI’s study abroad program #1 in the nation this year which portrays the impact these projects have had (Most Popular Study Abroad Program, 2017 ). Successful program sites such as the Copenhagen Project Center (CPC) have dramatically increased in student popularity, augmenting the demand for projects.
